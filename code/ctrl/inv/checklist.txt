INV module porting checklist

1. Scheduler integration
- Confirm REG_INIT and REG_INTERRUPT are available on the new platform.
- Confirm inv_ctrl_init, inv_ctrl_cal_theta, and inv_ctrl_isr are scheduled at the intended rates.
- Confirm CTRL_FREQ and CTRL_TS match the real control interrupt frequency.

2. HAL signal mapping in inv_hal.c
- Map inv_ctrl_hal.p_v_cap to the inverter capacitor voltage signal.
- Map inv_ctrl_hal.p_i_l to the inverter inductor current signal.
- Map inv_ctrl_hal.p_v_bus to the DC bus voltage signal.
- Map inv_ctrl_hal.p_set_pwm_func to the inverter PWM command function.
- Map inv_ctrl_hal.p_pwm_disable to a valid PWM shutdown function.

3. Signal semantics
- Confirm all mapped voltages use the same unit as the current implementation.
- Confirm all mapped currents use the same unit as the current implementation.
- Confirm p_v_cap and p_i_l polarity match the dq transform convention used by inv_ctrl.
- Confirm sampled voltage and current correspond to the same physical phase used by the controller.
- Confirm p_set_pwm_func(v_pwm, v_bus) uses the same interface meaning as the current project.

4. Timing and phase generation
- Confirm the commanded frequency range is compatible with inv_ctrl_limit_freq_hz.
- Confirm period and period_quarter remain valid for the supported frequency range.
- Confirm the sampling/update order keeps theta, sampled buffers, and dq conversion aligned.
- Confirm startup phase initialization does not introduce an output jump.

5. Hardware parameters
- Check code/ctrl/pfc_hardware.h and update all hardware constants for the new power stage.
- Confirm CAP_INV_VALUE matches the inverter output capacitor.
- Confirm L_PFC_VALUE matches the controlled series inductance used by the current-loop model.
- Confirm DC-bus range is compatible with the commanded PWM voltage.

6. Control parameters
- Check code/ctrl/inv/inv_ctrl.h for loop bandwidth, phase margin, and limit settings.
- Review d-axis and q-axis voltage-loop parameters.
- Review d-axis and q-axis current-loop parameters.
- Confirm the selected current-loop structure still matches the plant model.
- Confirm controller output limits are safe for the hardware.

7. Setpoint and run control
- Confirm inv_cfg active/building synchronization is called before control uses new setpoints.
- Confirm rms_ref_v, rms_slew_vps, freq_hz, and freq_slew_hzps have valid bounds.
- Confirm run_allowed gates PWM output as intended.
- Confirm prepare_run reinitializes controller states before enabling power stage operation.

8. ADC and sensing chain
- Confirm sensing scale and offset are calibrated before enabling the controller.
- Confirm sampled signals are updated before the control ISR runs.
- Confirm voltage and current samples are synchronized closely enough for dq conversion.
- Confirm noise and delay in the sensing chain are acceptable for the configured loop bandwidths.

9. PWM behavior and safety
- Confirm p_pwm_disable leaves the inverter in a safe state.
- Confirm PWM modulation range and saturation behavior are safe.
- Confirm vpwm command polarity matches the hardware driver convention.
- Confirm stop, fault, and restart behavior are safe and repeatable.

10. Bring-up verification
- Verify controller can start with zero unexpected PWM jump.
- Verify output frequency ramps to the target correctly.
- Verify output RMS reference ramps to the target correctly.
- Verify d-axis voltage loop regulates to the commanded magnitude.
- Verify q-axis voltage loop converges near zero in steady state.
- Verify d-axis and q-axis current loops track their references with correct polarity.
- Verify dq reconstruction from buffered samples is consistent across the full frequency range.
- Verify no buffer overrun or period underflow occurs at frequency limits.
